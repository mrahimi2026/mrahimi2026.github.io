How to Contact Your Members of Congress

A simple, step-by-step guide for U.S. constituents

This page explains how to find and contact your U.S. Senators and House Representative to share your views on issues that matter to you.

Step 1: Find Your Senators and House Representative

Every U.S. resident is represented by:

  • Two U.S. Senators
  • One U.S. House Representative

Use the official Congress website to find your elected officials by entering your ZIP code:

https://www.congress.gov/members/find-your-member

Step 2: Find Email and Phone Contact Information

After locating your Senators and Representative, click on each name. You will be taken to their official profile and website.

On their official website, you can usually find:

  • A contact form or email address
  • Phone numbers for Washington, D.C. and local district offices

You can also use these official directories:

Senate contact list:
https://www.senate.gov/senators/senators-contact.htm

House contact list:
https://www.house.gov/representatives/find-your-representative

Step 3: Use a Sample Email

Most offices prefer brief, respectful messages. You can copy and customize the example below. Including your ZIP code helps verify that you are a constituent. 

Subject: Constituent Concern Regarding [Issue]

Dear Senator/Representative [Last Name],

My name is [Your Name], and I am a constituent from [City, State].

I am writing to express my concern regarding [briefly describe the issue].

[ZIP Code]

Step 4: Follow Up (Optional)

Phone calls can also be effective. Staff members typically take brief messages and share them with the representative. 

Hello, my name is [Your Name], and I live in [City, ZIP Code].
I am calling to express my concern about [issue].
Thank you for your time.
